## Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers attempt to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or personal data by pretending to be a trusted source. These attacks usually occur through emails, messages, or fake websites that appear legitimate.

A typical phishing incident begins when a user receives an email or message that looks like it is from a bank, social media platform, or a well-known company. The message often creates urgency, such as warning about account suspension or offering a reward. It usually contains a link that directs the user to a fake website designed to look identical to the original one.

The process generally follows these steps:
1. The attacker sends a deceptive message posing as a trusted organization.  
2. The victim clicks on the provided link.  
3. The victim enters login credentials or personal information on the fake website.  
4. The attacker collects this data and uses it for unauthorized access or fraud.  

Students and working professionals are often targeted due to their frequent online activity and reliance on digital platforms.

The consequences of phishing can include unauthorized access to accounts, financial loss, identity theft, and loss of sensitive data. In many cases, victims are unaware until significant damage has already occurred.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious with unexpected messages, verifying sources, and avoiding clicking on suspicious links.
